Proposals

1. Technical Week

Objective: The General Championship has become more competitive over the years; however, it is observed that the technical output from the General championship is not that significant. In the recent years, there has been a significant increase in the number of student intake in IIT Kharagpur so in order to provide them with an opportunity to enhance and exhibit their technical skills; I propose to introduce a series of workshops specially conducted for the first year students. These workshops will be conducted in assistance of the various societies in the campus. Hence we would be able to encourage the first year students to go ahead and participate in the technology general championships as well. Through this proposal, I aim to increase the participation in technology general championship and provide the students of the first year an opportunity to enhance and exhibit their technical skills. This would also help to increase the postgraduate participation as it would expose them to the pattern of the events conducted in the General Championship.

3. Also this proposal will ensure that the quality of participation in the General Championship will improve over the years.

4. It will also help the different societies under the gymkhana to tap potential among the first year students.

2. Model Village

Objective:The organisational forms have undergone a constant process of evolution which has enabled them to meet part of the growing demand for social, community care and collective services. The Hult Prize is the world’s largest student competition for social good. The annual, year­long competition crowd­sources ideas from MBA and college students after challenging them to solve a pressing social issue around topics such as food security, water access, energy, and education. Through its structured approach to defining the challenge and local, regional and global competition rounds, the Hult Prize actively seeks to create a new kind of social business. I propose to conduct a similar event “Model Village” to enhance the growth of local communities around IIT Kharagpur. Hence, its purpose is to add value to the community by solving problems that exist and promote startup culture in campus. This event is exclusively for IIT Kharagpur students.

3. Identifying Potential KTJ Candidates

Objective : During December, participants are contacted by team Kshitij for participation and submission in events. This happens through calling in the whole database. If there exists a mechanism which will decide and tell us the potential participants, we can get the desired participation in a more productive way. Algorithmic training helps one identify the target data set and reduce the complex and tedious taking into account all the possible edge cases. This gives one a quick solution which can be modified upon. I propose to identify potential participants and improve productivity and quality of the event participation through user rating mechanism with the help of algorithm design by Jon Kleinberg.
